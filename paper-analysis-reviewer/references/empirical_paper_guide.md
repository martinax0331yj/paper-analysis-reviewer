# Empirical Paper Guide

Use this checklist for management, communication, publishing studies, sociology, economics-adjacent, and other empirical social science papers.

## 1. 概念界定

- Identify the theoretical origin of each core concept.
- Check whether the definition is stable across introduction, theory, hypotheses, measurement, and conclusion.
- Distinguish theoretical concept, observable indicator, questionnaire item, proxy variable, and constructed index.
- Do not treat a questionnaire item or database indicator as the concept itself.

## 2. 理论机制

- Reconstruct the mechanism connecting independent, dependent, mediating, moderating, and control variables.
- Check whether each hypothesis follows from theory rather than from statistical convenience.
- Ask whether mediation, moderation, moderated mediation, or chain mediation is theoretically necessary.
- Watch for mechanical model stacking without conceptual gain.

## 3. 变量测量

- Verify whether measurement items or indicators cover the concept's key dimensions.
- Check reliability, validity, source credibility, and whether reverse-coded or formative/reflective indicators are handled properly.
- Ask whether the operationalization narrows, shifts, or distorts the original concept.
- Separate measurement validity from statistical significance.

## 4. 样本与数据

- Check sample source, sampling process, time window, industry/region/platform boundaries, missing values, and exclusion rules.
- Ask whether the sample represents the theoretical population.
- Identify self-selection, survivorship bias, platform bias, recall bias, and common method source risk.

## 5. 研究设计

- Match the design to the research question: cross-sectional survey, panel data, experiment, quasi-experiment, case-coded dataset, text data, archival data, or mixed method.
- Check whether temporal order supports the proposed mechanism.
- Ask whether the design can support descriptive, correlational, mechanism, or causal claims.

## 6. 模型设定

- Check whether the model form matches the dependent variable and data structure.
- Inspect controls: each control variable should have theoretical or design-based justification.
- Watch for bad controls, post-treatment controls, multicollinearity, overfitting, and inappropriate fixed effects.
- Ask whether clustered, robust, or hierarchical errors are needed.

## 7. 因果识别

- Do not write correlation as causality unless the design supports it.
- Check endogeneity risks: omitted variables, reverse causality, simultaneity, selection bias, measurement error, and unobserved heterogeneity.
- For causal claims, identify the source of exogenous variation or the identification assumption.
- For IV, DID, RDD, PSM, synthetic control, or experiments, inspect the design-specific assumptions.

## 8. 稳健性

- Robustness should test whether the conclusion survives reasonable alternative choices.
- Look for alternative variable definitions, alternative samples, alternative models, placebo tests, lagged variables, additional controls, and sensitivity analysis.
- Do not treat many similar regressions as real robustness if they do not challenge the core assumption.

## 9. 异质性

- Heterogeneity tests should have theoretical motivation.
- Check whether subgroup divisions are pre-theorized or data-mined.
- Ask whether the paper explains why the mechanism differs across groups, contexts, platforms, periods, or institutions.

## 10. 外部效度

- Identify the scope conditions of the conclusion.
- Ask whether findings travel across time, region, platform, industry, organization type, culture, and sample composition.
- State where the conclusion should not be generalized.

## 11. 常见审稿质疑

- The paper equates statistical significance with theoretical contribution.
- The paper turns correlation into causality.
- The paper uses questionnaire items as if they fully represented theoretical concepts.
- The theoretical mechanism is thin, but the empirical model is complex.
- Controls are included mechanically without justification.
- Mediation or moderation is added because it is publishable, not because theory requires it.
- Robustness checks repeat the same assumption rather than challenging it.
- The conclusion exceeds what the data can support.
- The paper does not distinguish main effect, mechanism evidence, causal identification, and practical implication.
