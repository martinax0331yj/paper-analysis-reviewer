# Humanities and Social Science Guide

Use this checklist for humanities, publishing studies, communication studies, cultural studies, media studies, interpretive social science, theoretical essays, and text/material-based papers.

## 1. 概念谱系

- Trace where the core concept comes from, how it evolved, and which theoretical tradition it belongs to.
- Check whether the paper uses the concept in a way consistent with its genealogy.
- Identify concept drift: the paper starts with one meaning but later uses another.

## 2. 理论传统

- Identify the theoretical tradition, school, or debate the paper enters.
- Ask whether the paper actually dialogues with that tradition or only borrows vocabulary.
- Check whether rival theories or alternative interpretations are acknowledged.

## 3. 问题意识

- Distinguish topic, object, phenomenon, and research problem.
- A good paper should not merely say "this topic is important"; it should explain what existing explanation cannot resolve.
- The central question should guide concept selection, material selection, section order, and conclusion.

## 4. 材料与证据

- Check whether historical materials, texts, cases, policies, interviews, archives, or media examples are sufficient for the claim.
- Avoid using material piles as a substitute for theoretical explanation.
- Ask whether each piece of material plays an evidentiary role or is merely illustrative.

## 5. 章节结构

- Each section should answer a different layer of the same core question.
- The outline should show problem progression, not a flat list of themes.
- Check whether the order moves from concept definition to mechanism, context, evidence, interpretation, and implication.

## 6. 段落推进

- Each paragraph should have one claim, evidence, explanation, and transition.
- Check whether adjacent paragraphs repeat the same claim with different wording.
- Identify jumps from description to judgment without intermediate reasoning.

## 7. 语言文字逻辑

- Prefer precise concepts over grand labels.
- Avoid synonym repetition, empty intensifiers, and broad judgments without evidence.
- Check whether key terms are defined before being used.
- Inspect whether sentences make causal, normative, historical, or interpretive claims and whether the evidence matches that claim type.

## 8. 常见问题

- Concept listing without analytic relations.
- Synonymous repetition presented as theoretical depth.
- Macro judgment replacing evidence.
- Material accumulation replacing interpretation.
- Theory and material disconnected.
- Conclusion overextends beyond the analyzed materials.
- Sections are theme-parallel rather than question-progressive.
- The introduction promises a theoretical contribution but the body only describes phenomena.

## 9. 修改建议

- Recast the title and introduction around a precise question.
- Define the central concept and remove secondary concepts that do not do analytic work.
- Make the outline progressive: background problem, conceptual lens, material analysis, mechanism/explanation, conclusion boundary.
- For every section, write the question it answers and delete content that does not answer it.
- Replace broad claims with traceable evidence.
- Add rival explanations and explain why the paper's interpretation is stronger.
- Tighten language by removing repeated adjectives, slogan-like phrases, and unsupported generalizations.
